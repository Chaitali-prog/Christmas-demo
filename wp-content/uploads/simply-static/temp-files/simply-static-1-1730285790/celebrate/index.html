The Reason Why We Celebrate Christmas :

Christmas is celebrated to remember the birth of Jesus Christ, who Christians believe is the Son of God. The name ‘Christmas’ comes from the Mass of Christ (or Jesus).

A Mass service (which is sometimes called Communion or Eucharist) is where Christians remember that Jesus died for us and then came back to life.

  • Commemorating the Birth of Jesus Christ.
  • Spreading the Message of Love and Compassion.
  • Creating Traditions and Building Memories.
  • Fostering Togetherness and Family Bonding.
  • Bringing Light to the Winter Darkness.
  • Honoring Cultural Diversity.
  • Promoting Peace and Unity.
